Five Mistakes Brands Make During Product Launch

Published March 22, 2025 • 10 min read

Launching a new product is exhilarating, but many brands stumble due to avoidable errors. Here are the five most common mistakes – and how to steer clear of them.

1. Poor Timing: Launching during a holiday season when consumers are distracted, or during a competitor's major campaign, can bury your product. Research the calendar and choose a window with minimal noise and maximum attention.

2. Inadequate Market Research: Assuming you know what customers want without testing leads to mismatched features or pricing. Conduct focus groups, surveys, and test markets to validate your concept before the full rollout.

3. Weak Messaging: A confused consumer never buys. If your value proposition isn't clear and compelling across all channels, the product will fail to gain traction. Craft a simple, benefit‑focused message and repeat it consistently.

4. Ignoring Feedback During Soft Launch: Many brands rush to scale without listening to early adopters. Complaints about packaging, taste, or usability should be addressed immediately – they are gold for improvement.

5. Lack of Post‑Launch Follow‑Up: The launch is just the beginning. Failing to maintain momentum with continued marketing, influencer engagement, and retail support allows the product to fade into obscurity. Plan a 90‑day post‑launch campaign.

Beyond these five, brands also underestimate the power of experiential marketing during a launch. A product that people can touch, taste, and experience creates instant advocates.

Another oversight is neglecting trade partners. Retailers need to be convinced and supported; otherwise, they won't push your product.

Digital readiness is also crucial. A website crash or insufficient inventory due to poor forecasting can ruin a launch.

Finally, brands often forget to align internal teams. Sales, marketing, and customer service must all be on the same page to handle inquiries and capitalize on opportunities.
